## The First Few Hours

### Bandage Removal

Your artist will cover your new tattoo with either:
- **Plastic wrap/bandage**: Remove after 2-4 hours
- **Second skin (Saniderm/Tegaderm)**: Leave on for 3-5 days unless otherwise instructed

Follow your artist's specific instructions for your bandage type.

## Cleaning Your Tattoo

### First Wash

1. Wash your hands thoroughly with antibacterial soap
2. Gently remove the bandage
3. Using lukewarm water and fragrance-free antibacterial soap, gently wash the tattoo
4. Use your fingertips only—never use a washcloth or loofah
5. Pat dry with a clean paper towel (not a cloth towel)
6. Allow to air dry for 5-10 minutes before applying moisturizer

### Ongoing Care

- Wash your tattoo 2-3 times daily for the first two weeks
- Always use fragrance-free, dye-free soap
- Never scrub or pick at your tattoo

## Moisturizing

### When to Start

Begin moisturizing after your first wash, once the tattoo is completely dry.

### Recommended Products

- Fragrance-free, dye-free lotion (Lubriderm, Eucerin, Aveeno)
- Aquaphor (use sparingly for the first few days)
- Coconut oil (after initial healing)

### Application

- Apply a thin layer—your tattoo should not look shiny or wet
- Moisturize 2-3 times daily or whenever the tattoo feels dry
- Too much moisturizer can clog pores and cause breakouts

## What to Avoid

### For the First 2-3 Weeks

- **Swimming**: No pools, hot tubs, lakes, or oceans
- **Soaking**: Avoid baths and long showers—quick showers only
- **Sun exposure**: Keep your tattoo out of direct sunlight
- **Picking or scratching**: Let the peeling happen naturally
- **Tight clothing**: Wear loose, breathable fabrics over the area
- **Gym/heavy exercise**: Avoid excessive sweating for the first week
- **Sleeping on the tattoo**: Try to keep pressure off the area

### Long-term Care

- **Always use sunscreen** (SPF 30+) on healed tattoos when exposed to sun
- **Keep skin moisturized** for vibrant, long-lasting color
- **Avoid tanning beds** which can fade and damage your tattoo

## The Healing Process

### Days 1-3: The "Open Wound" Phase
- Redness, swelling, and tenderness are normal
- The tattoo may ooze plasma and excess ink
- Keep it clean and lightly moisturized

### Days 4-7: The Peeling Phase
- Skin will start to peel and flake
- **Do not pick or scratch**—let it happen naturally
- May feel itchy; moisturize to relieve itching

### Weeks 2-4: The Settling Phase
- Peeling should be complete
- Colors may appear dull or milky—this is normal
- Continue moisturizing and protecting from sun

### After 4 Weeks
- Your tattoo should be fully healed
- Colors will brighten and settle into final appearance
- Continue sunscreen and moisturizer for life

## Warning Signs

### Contact Your Artist or Doctor If You Experience:

- Excessive redness spreading beyond the tattoo
- Severe swelling or pain that doesn't improve
- Pus or foul-smelling discharge (clear plasma is normal)
- Fever or chills
- Red streaks extending from the tattoo
- Raised, itchy bumps that persist (may indicate allergic reaction)

## Touch-Ups
